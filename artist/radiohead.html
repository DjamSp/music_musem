Artist

Radiohead

alternative rock / electronic rock / experimental rock

Radiohead is a British rock band from Oxfordshire. The band was founded in 1985, and its line-up has not changed since that time. Radiohead's style is traditionally defined as alternative rock, although at various stages the sound ranged from britpop to art rock and electronic music. Some tracks from the Kid A and Amnesiac albums belong to IDM, in addition, Kid A contains compositions in the style of minimal techno, fusion and ambient, while Amnesiac contains compositions with elements of blues and New Orleans jazz.

- Radiohead is considered one of the most popular rock bands of the 90s, according to data at the end of 2008, Radiohead albums have sold approximately 28 million copies. In 2005, Rolling Stone magazine ranked Radiohead 73rd on its list of the greatest musicians in history. All albums from The Bends to In Rainbows were nominated for the Grammy Award for "Best Alternative Album" (Radiohead won this nomination three times), all albums received gold or platinum status in the USA and Great Britain and were included in the list of "Thousands of one albums that you just have to listen to!" compiled by the Music Chain website.
