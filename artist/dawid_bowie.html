David Bowie

glam rock / soul / art rock

David Bowie (real name David Robert Jones; David Robert Jones, born January 8, 1947 in the London suburb of Brixton, died January 10, 2016) is a British rock musician. Bowie is called the "chameleon of rock" because of his ability to anticipate and even create musical trends, as well as constantly change his image, sometimes completely recreating himself. The very first appearance in show business took place in 1964, with the release of the single "Liza Jane / Louie, Louie Go Home". After this single, released under the name Davie Jones in collaboration with The King Bees, David released 6 singles from 1964 to 1966, recorded a large amount of studio material, and became BOWIE in 1966. In the same year, his first album, or to be more precise, the complete edition of the singles released by Pye, "Don't Be Fooled By The Name", was released. In June 1967, the full-length debut album "David Bowie" was released.

Bowie declared himself with the 1969 single "Space Oddity" and gained cult status thanks to the concept album "The Rise And Fall Of Ziggy Stardust And The Spiders From Mars" in 1972.

Throughout his career, Bowie continued to innovate, exploring various genres including electronic, industrial, and jungle. His final album, "Blackstar", was released on his 69th birthday, just two days before his death from liver cancer. The album was met with widespread critical acclaim and is considered one of his greatest works.

Bowie's influence extends far beyond music into fashion, art, and culture. He was known for his androgynous appearance, especially in the early 1970s when he performed as the alien rock star Ziggy Stardust. His constant reinvention and chameleon-like ability to transform himself made him a cultural icon.

Over the course of his career, Bowie sold an estimated 140 million records worldwide. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1996. In the UK, he was awarded nine platinum album certifications, eleven gold and eight silver, and released eleven number-one albums. In the US, he received five platinum and nine gold certifications.
