Sombr

indie / alternative / rock

Shane Michael Booz (singer's real name) was born on July 5, 2005 in New York City, and grew up on the Lower East Side with his sister. Parents are Benna Serfati and Andy Booz.

Shane's house was filled with creativity. His father often sat down at the piano or picked up a guitar, and the boy literally absorbed the music. At the age of 10, he wrote his first song, and at 11 he started experimenting with GarageBand on his phone. Later he switched to the computer version of the program, and then mastered Logic Pro, where he made his first rap beats.

Read more

During the COVID pandemic, the young man spent nights in his room honing his producing skills and creating demos. At the same time, he studied vocal at LaGuardia High School of the Arts, where he performed arias in Italian and German, studied breathing and voice production. According to him, this greatly strengthened his vocal abilities and allowed him not to lose his voice even during long concerts.

The singer chose the pseudonym sombr (translated from English as "gloomy") intentionally, leaving all letters lowercase. It's a reflection of vulnerability, emotions, and inner state. And the letters of his full name, too.

The first turning point was the song Caroline (2022). Shane wrote it in high school, where he was bullied by his classmates. He posted the track and clip online late at night, and the next morning the guy's life changed: the mail was inundated with letters from record labels. As a result, sombr signed a contract with Warner Records.
