Artist

Kate Bush

art-pop / baroque-pop / alt-rock

Kate Bush is a unique and distinctive phenomenon in the world of music, whose work is difficult to fit into a single genre. She is often called the "queen of art-pop," and this title reflects the essence of her art — she uses piercing melodies, complex arrangements, and deep, almost literary lyrics with equal virtuosity.

Her journey began unusually early. She started composing music at the age of 11, and at the age of 16, her demos came to the legendary Pink Floyd guitarist David Gilmour. He was so impressed that he helped pay for the studio sessions and introduced her to the EM Hall on 19th May, at the age of 19, the world heard the single "Wuthering Heights". This song became a sensation: not only because of Kate's incredible, almost operatic vocals and complex musical structure, but also because it was arguably the first ever British No. 1 song written and sung by a female author.

Kate embodied the image of the heroine of Bronze Song, Catherine Earnshaw, in her very first video, creating a standard of her style — dance as a form of storytelling, emotional gestures and a mystical atmosphere.

In 1973, one of these cassettes fell into the hands of David Gilmour of Pink Floyd, who found the young singer's work quite interesting and offered her his help in making a better recording. First, he provided Katie with a semi-professional tape recorder and microphones, using which she re-recorded some of her songs, and then invited her to his home studio, where they recorded about a dozen more tracks in a day (Gilmour himself accompanied on guitar and invited two musicians from the band Unicorn to perform bass and drums). Some of these recordings were planned to be released on the Kate Bush — The Early Years CD in 1986, but the entire trial run was destroyed at the insistence of Kate Bush herself, who felt that this material did not deserve to be published.

Finally, in 1975, the same David Gilmour arranged for Katie to record in a professional studio, paying for the services of musicians, an arranger and a sound engineer. In total, three tracks were recorded.: The Man With The Child In His Eyes, The Saxophone Song and Maybe (the first two were later included on the album The Kick Inside). The management of EMI liked this demo recording and Katie was offered a contract, which was signed in 1976. Apparently, EMI at that time believed that it was too early for Bush to start a professional music career, but they offered her favorable enough conditions so that the promising performer would not be intercepted by competitors.
